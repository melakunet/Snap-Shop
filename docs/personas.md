# User Personas

Research method: secondary research — analysis of public App Store and Google Play reviews for five comparable apps (Google Lens, Amazon Lens, Pinterest Lens, CamFind, OneScan). Critical and negative reviews were weighted to surface unmet needs. Findings are reflected in the pain points and goals below.

---

## Persona 1 — The Deal-Hunter

**Who they are**
A price-sensitive shopper, typically 22–38 years old, who regularly browses discount stores, thrift shops, and online marketplaces. They flip items, hunt clearance aisles, and treat finding a lower price as a personal win. They are comfortable with technology and expect near-instant results.

**Goals**
- Know instantly whether a price in front of them is genuinely good or can be beaten online.
- Compare across multiple retailers without switching apps.
- Revisit saved scans to catch price drops before buying.

**Pains — from competitor research**
- Amazon Lens only shows Amazon prices. Deal-hunters need cross-retailer comparison and they know it. (Amazon Shopping app reviews, AppFollow)
- Sponsored and promoted results make them distrust the ranking — they cannot tell if the "best match" is paid placement or genuinely cheapest. (Amazon Shopping app reviews)
- Apps like CamFind that once worked well have degraded; deal-hunters have already churned from multiple tools and arrive sceptical. (CamFind App Store, 2/5, Oct 2024)
- OneScan's barcode-only approach means items without a readable code (damaged tags, second-hand goods) cannot be scanned.

**Relevant quote from research**
> "search results are not accurate all the time" — Balwinder B., Google Lens review, SourceForge

## Persona 2 — The Gift-Buyer

**Who they are**
A shopper, 28–50, who buys gifts for others and needs to match something they have seen — in a shop window, in a photo, or on someone else. They do not always know the brand name or exact model. They want to identify the item and find where to buy it at a reasonable price, ideally with a direct link they can share or act on.

**Goals**
- Identify an item from a photo (their own or one someone sent them) without knowing the brand.
- Find a reliable place to buy it with a working link that goes to the right product page.
- Complete the task in one app, not by hunting across multiple websites.

**Pains — from competitor research**
- Pinterest Lens identifies style categories but product links frequently break or redirect to the wrong page. Gift-buyers need a reliable deep link, not a homepage. (Capterra reviews, capterra.com/p/234608/Pinterest/reviews/)
- AI-generated images in Pinterest search results make it hard to distinguish real, purchasable products from synthetic imagery — a confusion point for someone trying to match a specific item. (TechCrunch coverage, 2025)
- Google Lens shopping results skew toward big brands; a unique or artisan item seen in a photo may not surface at all. (Product Hunt review)
- No app currently handles a photograph of an item that has no barcode (a piece of jewellery, a handmade product, an item in a shop window) reliably.

**Relevant quote from research**
> "It is hard to find the items they are marketing — sometimes you can't find the actual website." — Capterra reviewer, Pinterest reviews

## Persona 3 — The In-Store Shopper

**Who they are**
A practical adult, 30–55, who shops primarily in physical stores but wants to make sure they are not overpaying before they put something in their basket. They are not power users — they want something that works the first time, every time, without reading a tutorial. Speed and simplicity are everything; a result that takes more than a few seconds feels broken.

**Goals**
- Point their phone at a product and get a price comparison in seconds.
- Not have to find a barcode — just point the camera at the product.
- Trust the results enough to make a decision on the spot.

**Pains — from competitor research**
- Amazon Shopping app's filter and search interactions are "horrifyingly slow" — 10–15 second delays while standing in an aisle are not acceptable. (AppFollow, App Store reviews)
- Google Lens misidentifies common household items; an in-store shopper who gets a wrong result once loses confidence immediately. (SourceForge, Charlie B., Jun 2023)
- CamFind failed to identify clearly labelled products — the one use case this persona needs most. (App Store, 2.3/5 overall)
- The Lens overlay in Pinterest is described as annoying even by casual users — this persona will simply delete an app that feels cluttered. (Quora community)

**Relevant quote from research**
> "far and away the lamest most frustrating app that I have ever tried" — Charlie B., Google Lens, SourceForge (Jun 2023) — reported after testing multiple household items in a real shopping scenario
