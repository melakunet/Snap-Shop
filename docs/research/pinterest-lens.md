# Competitor Research Notes — Pinterest Lens

**App:** Pinterest (Lens / visual search feature within the main app)
**Stores reviewed:** App Store (apps.apple.com/us/app/pinterest/id429047995), Capterra (capterra.com/p/234608/Pinterest/reviews/), Quora community (quora.com/How-do-I-turn-off-Pinterest-apps-annoying-Visual-Search), TechCrunch coverage
**Research focus:** Critical and negative user feedback on visual search, shopping results, and product-finding flows

---

## Recurring Pain Points

**1. AI-generated images degrade search quality**
Pinterest's visual search returns increasing numbers of AI-generated images alongside real products and photos. Users trying to find a real item to buy report having to scroll past synthetic images that look similar but link nowhere useful. Pinterest has acknowledged the problem and begun labelling AI content, but users describe the experience as already degraded.

**2. The Lens overlay icon is intrusive and hard to remove**
A small Lens / circle icon appears on every image in the feed and cannot be turned off on most device and app-version combinations. Users on Quora specifically sought out a way to disable it, describing it as "annoying" and visually distracting during normal browsing.

**3. Broken and redirected product links**
Once a user taps through from a visual search result to buy a product, the destination link frequently does not work, redirects to the retailer's homepage rather than the specific product page, or lands on a listing for a different item altogether.

**4. Scam ads mixed into shopping results**
Reviewers warn that Pinterest's shopping ecosystem contains a significant volume of fraudulent or low-quality seller ads. Users have been redirected to scam stores through visually appealing Lens results.

**5. No price comparison**
Pinterest Lens identifies a style or product category and connects users to retailers, but it does not show prices or rank results by cost. Users who want to find the cheapest option must open multiple tabs and check manually.

## Takeaways for Snap & Shop

1. Completing the purchase path matters as much as identifying the product — broken or vague links are as bad as wrong identification.
2. Show exact prices with direct retailer deep-links, not just brand name or category. Pinterest's gap here is Snap & Shop's core value.
3. Never mix AI-generated content into results — users are already sensitised to synthetic noise from Pinterest and will notice immediately.
4. Keep the camera UI clean — overlays and persistent icons that users cannot remove create lasting negative associations.
