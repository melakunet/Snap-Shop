# Top 10 User Needs — Ranked by Frequency

Research basis: pain-point analysis of public App Store and Google Play reviews across five competitor apps.
Frequency = number of apps (out of 5) whose review notes surface this need.
Ties broken by intensity and centrality of the complaint within each note.

---

| Rank | User Need | Frequency | Apps where this surfaced |
|------|-----------|-----------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Accurate product identification — correct result on the first scan, not a wrong guess | 5 / 5 | Google Lens, Amazon Lens, Pinterest Lens, CamFind, OneScan |
| 2 | Visual scanning that works without a barcode — camera-based identification of unlabelled, second-hand, or packaged items | 4 / 5 | Google Lens, Pinterest Lens, CamFind, OneScan |
| 3 | Cross-retailer price comparison in one place — no forcing the user to leave the app to check a second store | 4 / 5 | Google Lens, Amazon Lens, Pinterest Lens, OneScan |
| 4 | Results ranked by price only — no sponsored listings, no algorithm-boosted placements in the scan output | 4 / 5 | Google Lens, Amazon Lens, Pinterest Lens, OneScan |
| 5 | Privacy-respecting experience — minimal data sent, no photos retained on server, no ecosystem tracking | 4 / 5 | Google Lens, Amazon Lens, Pinterest Lens, OneScan |
| 6 | Fast results — scan-to-price in under five seconds for common products; visible progress indicator otherwise | 3 / 5 | Google Lens, Amazon Lens, CamFind |
| 7 | App stability — completed scan stays on screen; a crash or accidental back-tap does not lose the result | 3 / 5 | Google Lens, Amazon Lens, Pinterest Lens |
| 8 | Clean, uncluttered camera UI — no persistent overlays or forced AI assistants the user cannot dismiss | 3 / 5 | Google Lens, Amazon Lens, Pinterest Lens |
| 9 | Consistent quality across app updates — recognition accuracy must not regress after a release | 3 / 5 | Google Lens, Amazon Lens, CamFind |
| 10 | Working deep-links to the exact product page — tap a result and land on the right item, not a homepage | 2 / 5 | Amazon Lens, Pinterest Lens |

---

## Notes on ranking

**Rank 1** is unanimous: every app's negative reviews centre on the scan returning a wrong or useless result. CamFind's notes alone contain four separate quotes about misidentification; Google Lens has three.

**Ranks 2–5** each appear in 4 of 5 apps. Tie-breaking order reflects centrality to the core use case:
- Barcode-free scanning (#2) is the fundamental capability gap in CamFind and OneScan — without it, the whole product fails.
- Cross-retailer comparison (#3) is Amazon Lens's structural ceiling and OneScan's explicit limitation.
- Unsponsored ranking (#4) is called out by name in Google Lens, Amazon Lens, and Pinterest reviews.
- Privacy (#5) surfaces in 4 apps but as a secondary concern rather than the first reason users leave.

**Ranks 6–9** each appear in 3 of 5 apps. Tie-breaking order reflects the strength of user language: Amazon reviewers explicitly measure wait times in seconds (#6); crashes that lose a scan are described as session-ending (#7); Pinterest and Amazon overlays prompted dedicated community threads seeking removal (#8); CamFind's regression was documented by users across multiple years (#9).

**Rank 10** appears in only 2 apps but is included because a broken final step (the purchase link) cancels all value delivered by the scan — Pinterest reviewers specifically named it as the reason the tool is unusable for actual shopping.
