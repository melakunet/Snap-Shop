# Competitor Research Notes — OneScan: Compare Prices Fast

**App:** OneScan: Compare Prices Fast (by Fabmiro LLC)
**Stores reviewed:** App Store (apps.apple.com/us/app/onescan-compare-prices-fast/id6448856048) — 4.9/5 from 17 ratings; Google Play (play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.onescan.app)
**Research focus:** Identifying structural limitations and unmet needs despite positive early reception

---

## Recurring Pain Points

**1. Barcode-only scanning — no camera-based visual recognition**
OneScan requires a readable barcode on the product. It cannot identify an unlabelled item, a product photographed from the internet, a second-hand item without packaging, or anything that does not have a scannable code. This is the app's fundamental ceiling.

**2. Very limited retailer coverage**
Results are sourced from four retailers: Amazon, Walmart, Best Buy, and eBay. Categories like fashion, groceries, sporting goods, and home décor are thinly represented. Users wanting a price from Target, B&H, or specialty retailers are out of scope entirely.

**3. Small review volume makes reliability assessment difficult**
At the time of research, the App Store listing had 17 ratings. While the average is 4.9/5, a base this small can reflect only early adopters or personal networks rather than broad real-world use. There is no review data on edge cases, failure modes, or accuracy across product categories.

**4. Affiliate model creates potential result bias**
OneScan discloses it earns commissions from purchases made through its links. While the stated intention is user alignment, there is no public information on whether retailer coverage or ranking is influenced by commission rates rather than purely by lowest price.

**5. No visual or multimodal scanning**
A user who cannot locate or scan a barcode — for example, an item on a store shelf with a damaged tag, a product in a shop window, or something seen in a video — has no path forward with OneScan.

## Takeaways for Snap & Shop

1. OneScan demonstrates that a focused, no-friction price comparison experience resonates — its model (scan → ranked prices → buy) is close to Snap & Shop's results flow and validates the concept.
2. Visual recognition (no barcode required) is the decisive differentiator. Any product, anywhere, in any condition is the goal OneScan cannot reach.
3. Retailer breadth matters: covering Amazon, Walmart, Best Buy, eBay, and Target at minimum is table stakes. B&H, Kroger, and category-specific retailers add value in later iterations.
4. Transparency on ranking methodology will build trust — affirm explicitly that results are sorted by price, not by affiliate commission rate.
